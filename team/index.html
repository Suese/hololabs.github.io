The Hololabs Team

With professional backgrounds in AAA video games and interactive media development, the team at Hololabs has a passion for innovative new media technologies -- from free-to-play mobile games to interactive web applications, art installations and virtual reality.

 

Paul Warne designed levels in the videogame industry for over ten years on AAA franchises such as “Psychonauts”, “Assassin’s Creed”, “King King”, and “Splinter Cell” at Doublefine, Lucas and Ubisoft Studios. He is an award-winning video-installation artist and teaches videogame design at Champlain University.

Mike Wozniewski is a designer and software engineer with a background in intelligent systems. His research at institutions such as McGill’s Centre for Intelligent Machines and the Society for Arts and Technology has produced dozens of artworks, performances, and installations, and has been presented at several international conferences.

Geoffrey Cooper is an art guy with many talents. He creates concept art, 3d models, UI designs and a bit of animation too. He comes from a background in product design and interaction design, and he previously worked for Sony, Siemens and Fox Sports.

Astrid Rosemarin has worked in the TV and interactive digital media spaces in Toronto, Edmonton, and now Montréal. Before joining Hololabs she has worked at social media startup Epilogger, trade organisation Interactive Ontario, and the Bell Broadcast & New Media Fund and Independent Production Fund.

Stephen Asher is a game designer and programmer with ninja-like skills in Unity3D. He is also one of the founders and organizers of the Mount Royal Game Society, holding monthly meetups for the indie game community, and offering support to anyone who would like to try making a game.
